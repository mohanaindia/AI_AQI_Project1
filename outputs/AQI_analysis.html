🔍 Key Patterns Found
Auto-detected from 99,634 records across 110 stations
📅
Strong Seasonality
AQI spikes every Oct–Feb (winter) and drops to lowest in Jul–Sep (monsoon). This pattern repeats consistently across all years.
💨
PM2.5 Dominates
PM2.5 is the #1 driver in 40% of readings, followed by PM10 (35%). Together they account for 75% of all dominant pollutant days.
🗺️
North vs South Divide
Delhi (DL*) stations average AQI ~240+ (Poor), while southern Kerala (KL*) and Karnataka (KA*) average AQI ~50–65 (Good).
📉
COVID Lockdown Signal
April 2020 shows a sharp AQI drop to 80.7 — the lowest April on record — reflecting the COVID-19 lockdown's effect on emissions.
📈
Worsening 2018–2019
Winter peaks worsened from 2017 through 2019, with Dec 2018 hitting a dataset high of 271 avg AQI before improving in 2020.
🚗
CO as a Hidden Driver
CO is the dominant pollutant in 15,813 readings (~16%), signaling vehicular and industrial combustion as a major pollution source.

Why PM2.5, PM10 & CO Matter Most
PM2.5 — Critical
Particles <2.5µm penetrate deep into lungs and bloodstream. Dataset avg of 80.3 µg/m³ is 34% above India's 24hr safe limit. PM2.5 is the dominant pollutant in 40% of all station-days.
PM10 — High
Coarse particles from dust, construction, and road traffic. At 158 µg/m³ avg, this is 58% above the safe limit of 100 µg/m³ — pointing to significant dust and construction activity.
CO — Underrated
Carbon monoxide is dominant in 15,813 days (16% of readings). CO's dominance indicates heavy vehicular and biomass burning activity, especially in urban and peri-urban stations.
